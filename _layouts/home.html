About Me

I am a Computer Science PhD student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I am grateful to have prof. Frederic Sala and prof. Ramya K. Vinayak as my advisors. I study the fundamentals of machine learning. Before coming to Madison I worked at IBM Research, India and spent two wonderful years at the IISc, Bangalore to obtain my masters in CS. In my previous life I was a software engineer at Ittiam Systems, Bangalore for a couple of years where we built a media transcoding service.

Research Interests
I am broadly interested in understanding machine learning fundamentals and developing theoretically grounded solutions. Topics I have been working on : weak supervision, active learning, threshold-based auto-labeling, OOD robustness and calibration.

Checkout my work with awesome collaborators and feel free to connect if something strikes a chord.
