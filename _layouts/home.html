About Me

I am a Computer Science PhD student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I am grateful to have prof. Frederic Sala and prof. Ramya Vinayak as my advisors. I study the fundamentals of machine learning. Before coming to Madison I worked at IBM Research, India and spent two wonderful years at the IISc, Bangalore to obtain my masters in CS. I was a software engineer at Ittiam Systems, Bangalore for a couple of years where we built a media transcoding service. Here is my CV.

Research Interests
I am broadly interested in understanding machine learning fundamentals and developing theoretically grounded solutions. Topics I have been working on : weak supervision, active learning, auto-labeling, OOD robustness and calibration.
