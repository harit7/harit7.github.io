About Me

I am a Computer Science PhD student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I am grateful to have prof. Frederic Sala and prof. Ramya K. Vinayak as my advisors. My research interests are in machine learning theory, particularly in settings where labeled data are scarce and noisy. Before coming to Madison I worked at IBM Research, India and spent two wonderful years at IISc, Bangalore to obtain my masters in CS. I have also worked as a software engineer at Ittiam Systems, Bangalore for a couple of years where we built a media transcoding service.

Research Interests

— Weak Supervision, Active Learning, Auto Labeling.
— Understanding Deep Learning.
— Representation Learning and non-Euclidean ML
